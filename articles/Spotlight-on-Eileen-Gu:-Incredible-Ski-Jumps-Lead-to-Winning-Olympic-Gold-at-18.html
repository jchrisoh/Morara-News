Spotlight on Eileen Gu: Incredible Ski Jumps Lead to Winning Olympic Gold at 18
By Hanna Grover | Published Feb 13, 2022 10:40 p.m. PST
Eileen Gu is the youngest Olympic gold medalist for freestyle skiing, earning her gold medal at the 2022 Beijing Olympics. Eileen is Chinese-American and said the decision to compete for China at the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics over the U.S comes from her strong bond with her mom who grew up in Beijing, encouraging her to embrace her heritage and honor her ancestry. Eileen began learning how to ski at age three and went professional in 2020 at the age of 16. It's astonishing that she is only two years into her professional skiing career and she is the youngest gold medalist for freestyle skiing.

Having that title is immensely impressive, but in addition to that, Eileen landed a left double 1620, a trick she never attempted before, which brought her to gold in the 2022 Beijing Olympics. She became the second person to ever land this trick, beating French freeskier Tess Ledeux who landed the trick before her. In the leadup to the Olympics, Gu and Ledeux had been pushing the progression of womenâ€™s freeski big air by trading firsts. In December, Gu was the first female freeskier to land the double cork 1440 at a big air event in Colorado. A month later, Ledeux responded by landing the first-ever 1620 at the X Games.

The left double 1620 brought Eileen to gold in the Olympics and she was in disbelief, her first ever attempt being successful. She immediately broke out in joy after her extraordinary landing and her reaction was an outcome of all her hard work. Originally, before her awe-striking landing, Gu was in third place and she had one last jump to go. Her mother, Yan Gu, was at the bottom of the jump, trying to give her daughter advice. She told Eileen to do her 1440, referring to a trick that Gu had landed nearly perfectly already, in hopes of lifting her to silver. Eileen, being ambitious and willing to take risks, had different ideas. She knew Ledeux did a 1620 in her first run, and that motivation was all she needed to rise to the podium.

Although this was Gu's first Olympic gold medal, it certainly will not be the last. Eileen participated at the FIS Freestyle Ski and Snowboarding World Championships 2021, winning two gold medals in Freeski Halfpipe and Freeski Slopestyle. She became the first freeskier to win two golds at the FIS Freeski World Championship and won a bronze medal in Freeski Big Air.

Nonetheless, Eileen has a very successful career ahead of her, and this is merely the start of an incredibly gifted young skier.
