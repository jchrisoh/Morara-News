MBTI Personalities: What Personality Do You Have?
By Holly Li | Published Dec 13, 2020 7:52 p.m. PST
The Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) personality test is a popular test that categorises people into 16 personalities. The test is widely used by enterprises, academies, and at seminars. It has also sparked debates on social media. So, what exactly is the MBTI personality test? Is it truly reliable?

What is the MBTI Personality Test?
The MBTI personality test is made of four components: Introvert/Extrovert, Sensing/Intuition, Thinking/Feeling, Judging/Perceiving.

Extrovert/Introvert
People who are extroverts generally enjoy socializing and are outspoken. Extroverts do not like to be alone and obtain their energy through interactions with others. In contrast, people who are introverted prefer to speak less and do not feel comfortable in social environments. Introverts tend to enjoy being alone and consider interacting with others to be tiring or unnecessary.

Sensing/Intuition
People who receive the letter S, short for sensing, on the MBTI personality test are usually more practical. They rely on concrete facts when making their decisions. People who receive the letter I, short for intuition, on the MBTI personality test usually favor the theoretical world. Spiritual satisfaction is considered more important than that of reality.

Feeling/Thinking
People who receive the letter F, short for feeling, on the MBTI personality test emphasize the importance of emotional connection. They exemplify empathy and interpersonal skills. People who receive the letter T, short for thinking, on the MBTI personality test place more importance on logic. They believe in strictly following rules and thinking through multiple factors when coming to a decision.

Perceiving/Judging
People who receive the letter P, for perceiving, on the MBTI personality test tend to enjoy freedom and adventure. They tend to be flexible and spontaneous. People who receive the letter J, short for judging, on the MBTI personality test tend to eliminate uncertainty. They believe in the cause-and-effect relationship and rely on strategies.

Is the MBTI Personality Test Important?
The MBTI personality test is a popular tool for career planning and personal development. However, only a few of personality psychology research give definitive credit to the MBTI personality test due to its deficiencies. For example, the MBTI personality test categories people to two opposing sides despite most people typically being in the middle of the MBTI spectrum. Thus, the result can be inaccurate. In addition, when a person retakes the MBTI personality test, the test may provide different results. A lot of research papers have criticized the inaccurate nature of the MBTI personality test.

Nevertheless, MBTI can still be an effective tool for learning about different personalities. Analyzing the proportion of different traits in the result can help the test taker learn more about the strengths and weaknesses of their personality. A thorough understanding and analysis of the rationale behind the design of MBTI can reduce the imperfection of the test.

How to Take an MBTI Personality Test
https://www.16personalities.com/free-personality-test is the official website for the MBTI personality test. Access to the test and the results are completely free. It is suggested to take the test on the official website because the tests from other sources can be inaccurate. The test takes 10 minutes to complete. The questions are a set of statements that test takers can answer with either “agree” or “disagree”. If one is considering taking the test, one’s test results may be more accurate if one took the test without reading the traits of each personality because one might develop a predilection for a specific personality in that process.
