Return to Canada: Release of the Two Michaels
By Owen Hu | Published Sep 26, 2021 3:21 p.m. PST
After over 1,000 days of detention in China, Canadians Michael Kovrig and Michael Spavor have landed back in Canada. Despite being a seemingly simple event, the release of the Two Michaels marks a pivotal moment in a years-long tension between Canada and China, and is further complicated by the involvement of Huawei executive Meng Wanzhou.

First being detained in December of 2018, the both Michaels have since been crucial components to Canada-China relations. The detainment of the two are largely considered to be an act of retaliation from China after Huawei executive Meng Wanzhou was detained at Vancouver International Airport on December 1, 2018. The United States charged fraud against Meng on the basis that she was involved in US sanctions against Iran; she would be held in Vancouver for the next three years, and the two Michaels would go on to remain in detention facilities in China for the same period of time in what has largely been described as hostage diplomacy.

Following a lengthy extradition hearing for Meng and growing pressure for the release of the Two Michaels, events escalated into a crescendo in September of 2021. On September 24, the US Department of Justice declared that they had secured a deal with Meng. With her house arrest over, Meng left Canada and flew back to China later the same day. In a reversal of events since December of 2017, both Michaels were freed by the Chinese government mere hours later. China has reacted to the culmination of tensions by stating “No arbitrary detention of Chinese people is allowed” while denying they were engaging in hostage diplomacy. Meanwhile, Prime Minister Justin Trudeau has welcomed back the Michaels, commenting how “these two men have gone through an unbelievably difficult ordeal.”

The impact of these events means an uncertain relation between Canada and China in the future. For one, it is unsure whether Canada will ban Huawei’s 5G networks. The US, UK, and Australia have all imposed some sort of restrictions against the telecommunications giant. Additionally, Canada has stated that it is working to develop a long-term plan on its relation with China, and it is unknown how the recent events will affect such a strategy. Overall, the recent releases of both sides of the party represents a critical beginning in future relations between two of the world’s most influential nations, and could lead to further ramifications that will affect the geopolitical status of the entire world.
