Afghanistan in Crisis: What's Next?
By Owen Hu | Published Aug 23, 2021 3:17 p.m. PST
Chaos has descended upon Afghanistan. Mistrust and fear have spread to every corner of the nation; scenes of mayhem have surfaced online of confusion at Kabul International Airport; and the President of Afghanistan has fled to a faraway nation, leaving the government in a state of disarray. These recent escalations in tensions in Afghanistan, a result of the withdrawal of US troops and the subsequent advancement of the politically extreme Taliban, begs the question: what’s next for the country?

The simple answer is, it is entirely unclear. A variety of factions, such as international response, the reactions of Afghans, and the direction the Taliban take all could drastically influence the future of the nation. Additionally, the largely polarizing international reaction to the withdrawal of US troops, which many cited as the cause that allowed the Taliban to advance so far, has prompted intense diplomatic conflicts all throughout the world. Even the status of women’s rights is unclear–their role in Afghani society has yet to be decided, but it can be reasonably inferred that the change will undoubtedly mark a significant shift in everyday life. In short, the situation in Afghanistan has so many different components that, at this current stage in time, makes it impossible to plainly predict what is to come.

On the other hand, several predictions for Afghanistan are clear. For one, the Taliban has directly vowed to instill a system of government that is not democratic but instead based on Islamic principles and law. “[Democracy] does not have a base in our country,” a spokesman of the Taliban has said. Furthermore, it is certain that conflict and tensions will continue into the foreseeable future. With a median age under 20, the vast majority of the nation has little to no memory of the late 1990s, when the Taliban was last in power. As such, the recent Taliban takeover will be an entirely new experience for many Afghans, which may be a factor that will allow the Taliban to take advantage of and exacerbate confusion and uncertainty in the nation. Needless to say, the vulnerability of much of the nation creates a perfect breeding ground for further conflict and even violence.

The intricately complex situation of Afghanistan has allowed for much speculation on its future. Whether the world witnesses a nation plunged into a state of pandemonium or one where the Taliban embraces the values which many Afghans hold so dear has yet to be seen. Either way, it is in the best interest of the entire world to preserve peace and security in the region–that is a goal that all countries can work towards.
