Black Friday During COVID–19: What You Can Expect
By Holly Li | Published Nov 25, 2020 10:54 p.m. PST
Black Friday of 2020 takes place on Nov 27, the fourth Friday of November. In the past years, numerous people would line up in front of shops at midnight to purchase their desired items. However, due to the increase in coronavirus cases, B.C. has announced a mask mandate and a temporary social lockdown for the entire province. According to the announcement, all indoor and outdoor community and social events are suspended for the next two weeks, even if they are under 50 people. How will Black Friday be affected?

How Will This Year's Black Friday be Different From Past Years?
Under such restrictions, one might wonder: is Black Friday cancelled this year? The answer is no. However, retailers are making adjustments to accommodate the COVID-19 restrictions.

Difference 1: Online shopping is encouraged, as retailers are opting for more online deals rather than traditional, physical shopping.

Many stores are closed due to the pandemic, while those opening ones have safety protocols in place and limits on how many people will be allowed in the building at once. These factors make physical shopping less convenient and satisfying than shopping in the past years. Online shopping can be an alternative.

Difference 2: Deals start earlier this year and last for long duration than those in past years.

Due to increased demand of online shopping, businesses struggle to keep up with the surges of deliveries. Worse, because the air passenger traffic globally is down by more than 70 per cent compared to pre-pandemic times, delivering the packages from warehouses to consumers’ doorsteps become a headache for the businesses. To cope with the problem, retailers start to release early deals to alleviate the stress of commodity deliveries. Stores like Amazon have started the Black Friday discount since the start of November. Consumers are encouraged to shop early since the out-of-stock items or late packages will become potential problems of late purchases.

Pros and Cons of Online Shopping
Pros:
1. Lower Prices: Because many consumers are switching to online shopping, there will be more competing prices. If a consumer has already purchased a commodity and finds that the same commodity has a lower price on another website, he or she will likely return the first item and buy the cheaper one. In addition, due to the closure of many stores, fixed costs such as rent and workers’ wages can be eliminated, allowing sellers to offer lower prices on the websites.

2. Lower Risks: Lining up in the crowds is an especially risky decision since the stores are potential incubators of the coronavirus. Shopping online reduces the risks of contact with a mass number of strangers.

Cons:
1. Slower Delivery: Delivery is slower than usual during the holiday season due to an increased number of orders. It is not uncommon to wait for a month for an item. Overseas shipping might take longer time.

What Does This Mean to Retailers?
Retailers, especially small or independent ones, might suffer a loss of revenue due to the lock down. During the spring lockdown, small retailers already suffered a 20 per cent drop in revenue on average. Seeking a method to resolve the problem caused by the seemingly endless pandemic will be a challenge for the retailers.

Students' Perspectives
If one wants to shop in person on Black Friday, masks and socially distancing will be mandatory. Otherwise, one might not be able to enter a store. Moreover, students are encouraged to shop online rather than offline. A lot of deals and promotion codes can be easily found online, which makes the shopping experience easier and more efficient. Furthermore, when unwrapping your packages, do not forget to sanitize it because the coronavirus can live for hours to days on surfaces like countertops and doorknobs.
