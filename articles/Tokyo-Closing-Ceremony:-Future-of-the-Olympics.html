Tokyo Closing Ceremony: Future of the Olympics
By Owen Hu | Published Aug 11, 2021 6:24 p.m. PST
Despite the Japan National Stadium, built to allow nearly 80,000 spectators, holding virtually zero fans during the closing ceremonies of the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, fireworks still lit up the night sky in the Japanese capital on August 8th, 2021. Despite having a price tag of $15 billion, double the original budget for the games, the Tokyo Olympics were still seen as highly successful in offering a moment of international unity amidst a challenging global pandemic.

After a few subdued speeches were given and the traditional national anthems were played, the closing ceremonies saw live celebrations from the next host city of the Summer Olympics, Paris, the first time such an event has happened. The mayor of Paris, Anne Hidalgo, carried the Olympics flag from Tokyo all the way back to the French capital, while French President Emmanuel Macron waved to cameras atop the Eiffel Tower. Jets of blue, white, and red flew across the sky, painting the French flag onto the air. In only three years’ time, Paris will host the 2024 Olympics, the next summer games.

In a less-distant future, the next Olympic games will occur in only half a year, with Beijing set to host the 2022 Winter games. However, there has been notable controversy surrounding the 2022 games, such as a potential boycott due to China’s treatment of ethnic minorities. Even then, the games are set to go ahead, with Beijing becoming the first city to host both the Summer and Winter games, with the former being held 13 years ago in 2008.

After Paris, the next games are to be held in Milano Cortina d’Ampezzo in Italy in 2026, while the next summer games will be held in Los Angeles in 2028. The Australian city of Brisbane was recently chosen to host the 2032 Summer Olympics, which will be the third Summer Olympics that the country will host.

Interestingly, the host of the 2030 Winter Olympics has yet to be chosen. Although previous hosts, such as Sapporo in Japan and Salt Lake City in Utah have expressed interest in hosting the games, Vancouver has recently seriously considered entering a bid for the 2030 games, which will come two decades after the city last hosted the 2010 Winter Olympics. With domestic rivalry from other Canadian cities highly unlikely, it is still yet uncertain where the 2030 games will be hosted - the International Olympic Committee (IOC) will make that decision in 2023 in Mumbai, India.

Even with the challenging circumstances of the 2020 Olympic Games, the Olympics still went through, shining as a beacon of hope where the nations of the world converged at a single place with a common goal in mind. And even though the next Olympic games are expected to still be held with the COVID-19 pandemic in mind, it is only a matter of time before the future Olympic games see their stadiums filled once more with the cheers of tens of thousands of fans.
